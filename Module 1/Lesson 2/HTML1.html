What is Sustainable Devlopment Goals(SDGs)

Global Framework: A set of 17 interconnected goals established by the United Nations in 2015.

2030 Agenda: Part of the 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development, aimed at addressing global challenges.

Universal Call to Action: Aimed at all countries, regardless of their economic status, to promote prosperity while protecting the planet.

Focus Areas: Address critical issues such as poverty, inequality, climate change, environmental degradation, peace, and justice.

Interconnected Goals: Recognizes that progress in one goal can positively impact others, promoting a holistic approach to sustainable development.

Inclusivity: Emphasizes that no one should be left behind in the pursuit of sustainable development.

Timeframe: Goals are to be achieved by the year 2030, encouraging immediate and sustained action.

Measurement and Accountability: Each goal has specific targets and indicators for measuring progress and accountability.
